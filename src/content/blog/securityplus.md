I finally did it—I am now CompTIA Security+ certified!

This ended up being a long journey for me. I actually started studying for the SY0-601 version, but life happened, I had to take a break, and I just never came back to it. Time went by, and when I was finally determined to get certified, I started from scratch with the SY0-701.

## The Study Materials

My main resource was [Professor Messer's CompTIA Security+ SY0-701 Training Course](https://www.professormesser.com/security-plus/sy0-701/sy0-701-video/sy0-701-comptia-security-plus-course/). His videos are completely free and, in my opinion, have the best production quality and structure. I did purchase his [Practice Exams](https://www.professormesser.com/sy0-701-success-bundle/), which I found to be unmatched. I'm not affiliated with Professor Messer at all, but his practice exams were very close to the real thing, and the detailed explanations of all the question choices were extremely helpful.

Another resource I used was the [CompTIA Security+ SY0-701 Full Course, Labs, and Study Plan](https://www.udemy.com/course/comptia_security_plus/) on Udemy, and my feelings are mixed. I did not pay for this course as I have a business account through my job. I would say if you can access it for free, check it out, but I would not recommend purchasing it out of pocket. The videos were a nice change of pace, and it was helpful to listen to another person's perspective on the material. However, the production quality is lower compared to Professor Messer, and I cannot recommend the practice exams, as there were duplicate questions and the difficulty didn't feel right.

## Tackling the PBQs and Exam Day

You might be asking what I used to study for the PBQs (Performance-Based Questions). Honestly? I didn't prepare for them at all.

As for the exam itself, I was quite nervous because it's been many years since I've had to take a formal test. I took the common advice of skipping the PBQs and going straight to the multiple-choice questions, and I'm so glad I did. I didn't have as much time left as I thought I would, so I used what I had to get through everything and double-check all the questions I marked for review (which were a lot).

The PBQs were pretty foreign to me. I just tried to do the best I could and hoped for partial credit, since no one is really sure how CompTIA scores them. I ended up using every single minute allotted to me. I really didn't think I passed, so I was thrilled to see a passing score of 780 pop up after I clicked the finish button.

## What's Next?

I'm glad I finally accomplished what I set out to do originally. I have my eyes on [Hack the Box's CPTS](https://academy.hackthebox.com/preview/certifications/htb-certified-penetration-testing-specialist) next.
